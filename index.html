ABOUT ME

My name is Russell, but you can call me Russ. I currently live in the Sacramento, CA area and am a Northern California native. All throughout my life, I've loved the creative aspect of life; so at a very young age I began to play music and try my hand at a bit of drawing/sketching.

That creative interest, over years developed into my getting involved in graphic design. At first, just for fun, and then professionally as a freelancer. I've done many things professionally in my short adult life, but mostly I have been employed in the optical field/industry. I was a licensed optician and worked several years doing that until I began teaching it at a vocational college.

An opportunity opened for me to pursue my more creative interests and train as a software developer with Thinkful. So I jumped at the chance. Ever since I've loved combining my graphic design skills and web development skills to be apart some great projects. Some of which I hope you'll checkout below.

I believe my experience and skills would help foster an inclusive design and development process for any company that chose me for their team. I look forward and am excited to working alongside fellow technology professionals in producing great user experiences!

An app where individuals who enjoy reading and writing fan fiction can explore there creative interests. The user is able to create fictional universes off of their favorite characters and then share it with friends.

I wanted to build this because from a young age I've loved stories and story-telling. Imagination is important, so I created fanFIXion to make imagination a little more fun.

German Word Learner

An app where individuals can actively learn common words and phrases in the German language. The user can also keep track of their progress as they learn.

theSwattr

An app where a user can report any bugs/glitches on a given app and have a developer respond to the issue until resolved. The user can be a person reporting issues or a developer resolving issues.

As a team, myself and two other great developers decided on this app idea because we really wanted to focus on making something useful to the dev-community. We also strived to showcase some of our more complex skill-sets. You'll find that, especially on the backend.
